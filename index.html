The Platform

A Vision for Teller County

Dave’s plan is simple: keep our community safe, watch over your tax dollars, and protect the rural way of life we all love.

Integrity

Fiscal Responsibility & Transparency

Dave believes county government should live within its means, just like the rest of us. He is committed to making sure every dollar is accounted for and spent where it matters most.

  • Zero-Based Budgeting: Every tax dollar must be justified from the ground up each year to eliminate wasteful spending.
  • Transparent Audits: Continuing and publicizing regular, independent fiscal reviews to maintain absolute public trust in county finances.
  • Keeping Us Debt-Free: Having previously led the effort to wipe out Teller County's debt, and earmarking sufficient funds for the county to pay cash for the new Sheriff's Office Building, Dave will ensure we continue to build for the future with cash we’ve saved—not loans we can’t afford.
Protection

Keeping Our Community Safe

As a former Fire District President, Dave knows that safety in our mountains is about more than just response times—it's about staying ahead of the risks before they start.

  • Law Enforcement: Ensuring the Sheriff's office has the proper equipment and training to keep response times low and our communities safe.
  • Emergency Management, Fire and EMT Support: Dave has years of experience handling coordinated responses to emergencies, including mobilizing county assets to complement state, federal and local fire and ambulance services. With his leadership, Teller County will continue to produce exceptional results.
  • Robust Attention to Public Works: We all know that along with the dangers of criminal activity and wildland fires, navigating our many miles of rural roads, particularly during snow and ice events, can be deadly. Emphasizing proper maintenance and appropriate funding of our public works departments will always be a priority for Dave.
Legacy

Protecting the Land We Love

Protecting our mountains means finding the right balance between a growing community and the wild spaces we call home. Dave is committed to keeping Teller County feeling like Teller County.

  • Wildlife & Habitat: Protecting the Colorado rural wildlife and migration corridors that define our unique mountain environment.
  • Forest Health: Promoting active forest management and integrated fire-mitigation initiatives to protect our community assets. Incorporate robust private property mitigation opportunities with chipping equipment and slash/mulch sites.
  • Ranching Heritage: Standing firm for property and water rights to ensure our local ranching families can continue their legacy. He will also fully support 4-H to insure future generations will continue to live the Western experience.
Liberty

Defending Our Way of Life

Teller County decisions should be made in Teller County—not in Denver. Dave is a fearless advocate for local control and constitutional principles.

  • 2nd Amendment Sanctuary: Upholding Teller County’s status as a sanctuary for law-abiding gun owners against unconstitutional state mandates.
  • Fighting State Mandates: Resisting ridiculous policies from Denver that threaten our local businesses, water rights, land use and energy. Especially the "unfunded mandates" that continue to erode our freedoms.

Dave served two consecutive terms as Teller County Commissioner (2011–2019), providing eight years of dedicated public service. Advocacy • Legislative Leadership

Testifying at the State Capitol

It is often said that at the Capital, Teller County punches above its weight, and Dave kept up that tradition while serving as a Commissioner and beyond. He testified dozens of times at House and Senate committee hearings, and more importantly cultivated relationships with bill sponsors and Legislative Leadership.

Regardless of party, if a bill sponsor was proposing a new law that would directly impact Teller County, Dave was there to provide support or offer alternatives that ultimately worked to the benefit of his constituency.

Among his victories were Tax Increment Finance (TIF) modifications that broadened voter control of Urban Renewal Authorities, a pilot program for Pikes Peak Workforce Center that funded dedicated job training and placement for Veterans, and local control in Cripple Creek and Gilpin County for statewide online gambling.

His five years on the Colorado Counties Inc. (CCI) Board placed him on multiple steering committees for the National Association of Counties (NACo) allowing him to further the interests of Colorado and Teller County at the federal level.

Payment in Lieu of Taxes (PILT) which helps defray the cost to counties of maintaining emergency services in federally controlled areas (50% of Teller County) and limiting regulation of natural resources, were consistently items of discussion.

How & Where

Automatic Mail Ballots

Colorado is a mail-ballot state. All active registered voters will automatically receive a ballot at their listed address starting 22 days before the election.

Drop Box Locations

24-hour drop boxes are available at the Woodland Park Motor Vehicle Office, the Teller County Courthouse in Cripple Creek, and the Divide Market.

Register to Vote

Register online at the Secretary of State's website or in-person at any Clerk and Recorder office. You can register up to and including Election Day.
